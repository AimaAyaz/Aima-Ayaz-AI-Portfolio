## 🔹 Overview  
This assignment compares two major Natural Language Processing (NLP) libraries — **NLTK** and **spaCy** — focusing on their history, strengths, use cases, and performance differences. Both tools are widely used in academia and industry, but they serve different purposes in the NLP ecosystem.

---

## 🔹 Key Concepts  

### **NLTK (Natural Language Toolkit)**  
- Released in **2001** at the University of Pennsylvania  
- Designed for **education, research, and experimentation**  
- Includes extensive linguistic resources (WordNet, corpora, parsers)  
- Great for learning NLP fundamentals  
- Slower and more manual compared to spaCy  

### **spaCy**  
- Released in **2015** by Matthew Honnibal  
- Built for **production‑grade NLP applications**  
- Extremely fast due to optimized Cython backend  
- Uses **pre‑trained pipelines** for tokenization, POS tagging, NER  
- Ideal for chatbots, classification systems, and scalable deployments  

---

## 🔹 Comparison Summary  

| Feature | NLTK | spaCy |
|--------|------|--------|
| **Purpose** | Education, research | Production, real‑world apps |
| **Speed** | Slower | Very fast |
| **Pretrained Models** | Limited | Strong pretrained pipelines |
| **Ease of Use** | Great for learning | Great for deployment |
| **Best For** | Teaching NLP concepts | Building real applications |

---

## 🔹 Conclusion  
Both NLTK and spaCy play important roles in NLP development.

- **NLTK** is ideal for understanding algorithms and experimenting with linguistic concepts.  
- **spaCy** is optimized for speed, scalability, and modern NLP workflows.  

Together, they represent the bridge between **learning NLP** and **building NLP systems**.
